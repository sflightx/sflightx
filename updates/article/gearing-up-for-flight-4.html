OmegaSat 2-3 Mission on Flight 4 of Maya is set to launch on April 3, 12:30 UTC. Booster SN404 will attempt a soft targeted splashdown ~30km near the coast of DSC.

sFlightX is set to launch the OmegaLink 2-3 mission on the fourth flight of Maya. This payload, OmegaSat, "this satellite constellation will provide low-latency communications from any point of Earth" company says.

The vehicle involved this mission is Booster SN404, not a "not found" booster, but rather a fourth generation, and 04 in production line. Most structural upgrades are present here than Booster SN403, the third flight booster.

Booster SN403 did well, as it impacted the ground 45m/s with only collapsing the engine bay, and not exploding.

Due to payload limitations, this will carry another 1 OmegaSat to space. Future versions will improve, and will extend its payload capacity. Maya Heavy can launch 5 Sats in expendable mode.

The next flight is planned, with the first stage set to soft splashdown 30km downrange the launch site. This will enable us to make Maya a rapid reusable vehicle for the future.
